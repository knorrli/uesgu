**Three findings, all pointing the same way:**

1. **Coverage is COMPLEMENTARY, not subset.** Both `petzi-only` (~25 events the
   scrapers miss) and `scraper-only` (~48+ the PETZI agenda misses) are non-trivial
   and bidirectional. **PETZI is NOT a superset** — going PETZI-only would drop ~48+
   real events (kairo, sedel, kiff, dachstock, helsinki especially). → keep both.
2. **Times are highly reconcilable.** Real conflicts total ~11 across all venues,
   nearly all ±30 min; the dominant relationship is doors-vs-show (our scrapers often
   capture *doors*, PETZI gives *show*) — a fixed, reconcilable offset, not a clash.
3. **Genres are COMPLEMENTARY — the headline win.** Where one source has no genre the
   other usually does: PETZI fills Kofmehl (35/35) and most of KIFF; our scrapers fill
   Fri-Son (10/10) and Sedel (9). Where both have genres it's a granularity difference
   (PETZI coarse "Indie, Punk" vs our fine "Indie-Punk"), not a contradiction. Union =
   strictly richer.

**Recommendation:** **Keep both; merge with union semantics, PETZI as the stable
spine.** Dedup key = `venue + date + subset-aware fuzzy title` (proven here — the
subset rule matched truncated club-night titles like "Darkside" ↔ "Darkside: PYTHIUS…").
On a matched pair: union the genres; for time prefer PETZI's *show* and keep the
bespoke time as doors when it differs. Do NOT retire any of the 14 bespoke scrapers —
they each still catch events PETZI omits. Reconciliation cost is LOW and mechanical,
so the "too much to reconcile → pick one" threshold is **not** crossed.
